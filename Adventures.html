Exclusive Game Drive and Jungle Afternoon Tea

Taking the art of wilderness dining to a new level, our high-tea experience in the jungle is truly special. Our team will pick a beautiful spot with a scenic vista. Guests descend from their vehicles, stretch their legs and indulge in some exquisite creations from our pastry kitchen.

An exclusive game drive and jungle afternoon tea in Yala National Park is a once-in-a-lifetime experience. The point of this experience is to see some of the most iconic wildlife in Sri Lanka, including elephants, leopards, and crocodiles, in a safe and comfortable setting, and enjoy a leisurely afternoon tea in the jungle. The game drives are conducted in open-top jeeps, so you will have the best possible views of the animals. The guides are experienced and knowledgeable, and they will help you to spot the wildlife and learn about their behavior.

After the game drive, you will enjoy a leisurely afternoon tea in the jungle. You will be served a selection of finger sandwiches, scones, pastries, and cakes, along with tea or coffee. This is a great opportunity to relax and enjoy the peace and quiet of the jungle. This experience is perfect for anyone who wants to experience the best of Sri Lanka's wildlife and nature in a safe and comfortable setting, and enjoy a relaxing afternoon tea in the jungle.

Birding Experience at Bundala National Park

Bundala National Park’s wetland and saltern habitat is home to a large collection of resident birds, as well as migrants that visit every winter, from waders to raptors. Sightings of other key species such as elephants, crocodiles, wild boar, buffalo, spotted deer, and terrestrial birds can be expected.

Bundala National Park is a birdwatcher's paradise, located in the Southern Province of Sri Lanka. The park is home to over 200 species of birds, including both resident and migratory species. Some of the most common birds seen in the park include flamingos, pelicans, egrets, herons, spoonbills, ducks, and geese. The park is also home to a number of raptors, such as hawks, eagles, and owls. The best time to go birdwatching in Bundala National Park is during the dry season, from November to April. During this time, the water levels in the park are low, making it easier to spot the birds. The park is also less crowded during this time.

There are a number of ways to go birdwatching in Bundala National Park. You can either go on a guided tour or explore the park on your own. If you go on a guided tour, the guide will help you to identify the birds and point out the best places to see them. If you explore the park on your own, be sure to be respectful of the park and its wildlife. Some of the best places to go birdwatching in Bundala National Park include the mangrove swamp, mudflats, saltpans, lagoons, and forests. When birdwatching, it is important to be patient and quiet, so you don't scare the birds away. You should also use binoculars to get a closer look at the birds.

Guided Nature Walk

Explore the wilderness in and around Wild Coast Tented Lodge in the company of an expert ranger, who will point out visible animals as well as identify tracks of animals that have traversed our property recently. The combination of our ranger’s knowledge and the ability to walk through the forestry makes for the quintessential wilderness experience. A great experience for families.

Tented lodges guided nature walks are a great way to explore the natural beauty of Sri Lanka. Experienced guides lead these walks and will point out interesting features of the landscape and tell you about the plants and animals that you see. The walks are usually moderate in difficulty and can be enjoyed by people of all ages and fitness levels. On a tented lodges guided nature walk, you can expect to see a variety of plants and animals, including elephants, leopards, monkeys, and birds. You can also learn about the local ecology from experienced guides. In addition, you can get some exercise and fresh air, and enjoy the peace and quiet of the natural surroundings.

If you are looking for a fun and educational way to experience the natural beauty of Sri Lanka, then a tented lodges guided nature walk is a great option.
